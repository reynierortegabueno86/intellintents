## INTENTS

- opening: The user initiates or re-initiates the interaction (e.g., "Hello", "Good morning", "Hey there").
- closing: The user ends or concludes the current interaction (e.g., "Goodbye", "See you later", "Thanks, bye").
- phatic: The user maintains politeness or social connection without starting or ending the conversation (e.g., "How are you?", "Thank you", "Have a nice day").

## DISAMBIGUATION

- "Hello" / "Hi" / "Good morning" -> opening
- "Bye" / "Goodbye" / "See you" -> closing
- "Thank you" at the END of a conversation -> closing (wrapping up)
- "Thank you" in the MIDDLE of a conversation -> phatic (social lubrication)
- "How are you?" as a first message -> opening (greeting ritual)
- "How are you?" after an exchange -> phatic (social check-in)
- "Have a good day" -> closing
